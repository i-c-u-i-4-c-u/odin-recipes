Fried Rice

Description

Fried rice is a popular dish that originated in China but is now enjoyed in various cuisines around the world. It is made by stir-frying cooked rice with a combination of vegetables, proteins (such as meat, seafood, or tofu), and seasonings.

Ingredient

  • Cooked rice (preferably a day old and chilled)
  • Vegetables (e.g., carrots, peas, bell peppers, onions, corn, etc.)
  • Proteins (e.g., diced chicken, shrimp, beef, tofu, etc.)
  • Aromatics (e.g., minced garlic, grated ginger, etc.)
  • Eggs (optional)
  • Soy sauce or other seasonings (e.g., oyster sauce, fish sauce, sesame oil, etc.)
  • Cooking oil (e.g., vegetable oil, peanut oil, etc.)

Steps

  1. Heat a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add some cooking oil and allow it to heat up.
  2. If using eggs, you can scramble them in the pan first. Push the cooked eggs to one side of the pan.
  3. Add the aromatics, such as minced garlic and grated ginger, to the hot oil and saut√© them briefly until fragrant.
  4. Add your choice of vegetables and proteins to the pan. Stir-fry them until they are cooked through and tender. It's common to cook proteins first and set them aside before adding them back to the pan later.
  5. Push the vegetables and proteins to one side of the pan, creating a space in the center. Add a little more oil if needed.
  6. Add the chilled cooked rice to the center of the pan. Break up any clumps and stir-fry the rice with the other ingredients, ensuring it is evenly coated with the oil and evenly heated.
  7. Incorporate the vegetables, proteins, and rice together in the pan. Stir-fry everything together for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  8. Season the fried rice with soy sauce or other preferred seasonings, adjusting the amount to taste. Be cautious with salt if using soy sauce, as it is already salty.
  9. Continue to stir-fry for a few more minutes, ensuring the rice is heated through and well combined with the flavors.
  10. Remove the pan from the heat and serve the fried rice hot.
