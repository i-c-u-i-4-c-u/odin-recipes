Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a popular Italian dish that consists of layers of flat pasta sheets, sauce, cheese, and various fillings. The traditional version of lasagna typically includes a tomato-based meat sauce, such as Bolognese, made with ground beef, onions, garlic, and herbs.

Ingredient

For the cheese mixture:

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • Meat sauce (prepared separately, if desired)
  • 1 ½ cups (360 grams) ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup (100 grams) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups (200 grams) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until they are al dente. Drain the noodles and rinse them under cold water to prevent sticking.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, shredded mozzarella cheese, beaten eggs, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  4. If desired, prepare a meat sauce separately or use pre-made sauce.
  5. Spread a thin layer of sauce (meat or marinara sauce) on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish to prevent sticking.
  6. Place three lasagna noodles side by side on top of the sauce in the baking dish.
  7. Spread a layer of the cheese mixture evenly over the noodles.
  8. Spoon some sauce over the cheese mixture, spreading it evenly.
